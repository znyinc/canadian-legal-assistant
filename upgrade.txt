## What ChatGPT Does Well (The Gold Standard)

### 1. ACTION-FIRST STRUCTURE
ChatGPT leads with "what needs to be done" â€” numbered, prioritized steps.
- Step 1 is always the most urgent/immediate action
- Clear "Do this first, before X" prioritization
- Each step is self-contained and actionable

Current System: Leads with Classification, Forum Routing, Deadlines â€” INFORMATIONAL, not ACTIONABLE.

### 2. ROLE CLARIFICATION (Critical for Legal Newbies)
ChatGPT explicitly explains:
- "You are NOT prosecuting the case; the Crown Attorney is"
- "You are a witness, not a party"
- "This is YOUR role vs THEIR role"

Current System: Assumes user understands legal roles. Shows "Complainant" without explaining what that means.

### 3. DO / DON'T LISTS
ChatGPT includes explicit:
- "What you SHOULD do" (positive)
- "What you should AVOID" (negative)
- "Do NOT contact Riley directly"
- "Do NOT post publicly about the incident"

Current System: Only shows "What We CAN Do / CANNOT Do" for the SYSTEM, not guidance for the USER.

### 4. ANTICIPATION OF NEXT STEPS
ChatGPT explains:
- "Likely Legal Trajectory (High Level)"
- "Typical outcomes (vary by case)"
- "Anticipate the Defense (and How to Counter)"

Current System: Shows deadlines but doesn't explain what happens next or what to expect.

### 5. EMOTIONAL ACKNOWLEDGMENT
ChatGPT uses human language:
- "This is strongly recommended"
- "This directly supports your case"
- "Most cases resolve before trial"
- "Courts dislike cross-contamination"

Current System: Clinical, checkbox-style presentation. Lacks warmth.

### 6. PRACTICAL OFFERS
ChatGPT ends with:
- "If you want, I can: Draft X, Prepare Y, Help with Z"
- "Say which one you want to do next"

Current System: Shows "Generate Documents" button but doesn't explain WHAT documents or WHY.

### 7. EVIDENCE GATHERING GUIDANCE
ChatGPT includes specific evidence steps:
- "Photograph and video: fallen limb, break point, fungus..."
- "Save the June 2025 email (with headers if possible)"
- "Obtain contractor repair estimates"

Current System: Has Evidence tab but no contextual guidance on WHAT evidence matters for THIS case.

### 8. SETTLEMENT REALISM
ChatGPT includes:
- "Settlement Is Common (and Smart)"
- "Be open to: full payment, structured payment, insurance-funded settlement"

Current System: Only shows court-focused pathway. No mention of negotiation or settlement options.

## New Primary Output Format: "Your Action Plan"

Replace the current Classification-first view with an ACTION-FIRST structure:

### Proposed Layout Order:

1. **ACKNOWLEDGMENT BANNER** (New)
   - "We understand you're dealing with [situation type]. Here's what typically needs to happen next."
   - Empathetic, not clinical

2. **IMMEDIATE ACTIONS** (New Section)
   - "Do This First (Within 24-48 Hours)"
   - Numbered, prioritized steps
   - Specific to case type (criminal vs civil vs consumer)

3. **YOUR ROLE EXPLAINED** (New Section)
   - For criminal: "You are a witness, not the prosecutor"
   - For civil: "You are the plaintiff bringing the claim"
   - For consumer: "You have rights under Consumer Protection Ontario"

4. **EVIDENCE CHECKLIST** (Enhanced)
   - Case-specific evidence guidance
   - "Gather these items" with checkboxes
   - Priority indicators (critical vs helpful)

5. **IMPORTANT DEADLINES** (Existing, Repositioned)
   - Keep current format but add context
   - "This deadline matters because..."

6. **WHAT TO EXPECT** (New Section)
   - Likely trajectory / timeline
   - Common outcomes
   - Settlement vs trial statistics

7. **WHAT TO AVOID** (New Section)
   - "Do NOT" list specific to case type
   - Common mistakes to avoid

8. **YOUR OPTIONS** (New Section)
   - Multiple pathways with pros/cons
   - "Internal complaint â†’ Ombudsman â†’ Court" ladder
   - Settlement option always mentioned

9. **FORUM & ESCALATION** (Existing, Repositioned)
   - Move down from top â€” less prominent
   - Add "Why this forum?" explanation

10. **NEXT STEPS WE CAN HELP WITH** (New Section)
    - "Generate a demand letter"
    - "Create an evidence checklist"
    - "Prepare a victim impact statement"
    - Make it conversational: "Would you like us to..."

11. **CLASSIFICATION DETAILS** (Existing, Collapsed)
    - Move to collapsible section at bottom
    - Technical details for reference only

PART 6: ORGANIC FLOW STRUCTURE (Guide Role)
================================================================================

## The "Legal Navigator" Flow

The system should feel like a knowledgeable friend who:
1. ACKNOWLEDGES what you're going through
2. EXPLAINS what this means in simple terms
3. PRIORITIZES what needs to happen first
4. GUIDES you through each step
5. PREPARES you for what's coming
6. OFFERS to help with specific tasks

## Flow Implementation

### Step 1: Acknowledge
"You're dealing with [situation]. This can be stressful, but there are clear steps to take."

### Step 2: Orient
"Here's the big picture: This is a [criminal/civil/consumer] matter. That means [simple explanation]."

### Step 3: Prioritize
"First things first â€” here's what needs to happen in the next 24-48 hours:"

### Step 4: Guide
"Here's your step-by-step path forward:"
1. [Immediate action]
2. [Evidence gathering]
3. [Formal communication]
4. [Filing/reporting]
5. [Resolution options]

### Step 5: Prepare
"Here's what to expect: [timeline, outcomes, process]"

### Step 6: Offer
"We can help you with:
- Drafting a [demand letter / statement / form]
- Creating an evidence checklist
- Understanding your options"

PART 8: SUCCESS METRICS
================================================================================

## How to Measure Improvement

1. **Clarity Score**: Can a legal newbie understand what to do next?
2. **Action Score**: Are the first 3 steps immediately actionable?
3. **Empathy Score**: Does the output acknowledge the user's situation?
4. **Completeness Score**: Are all relevant pathways mentioned?
5. **Guidance Score**: Does the user feel guided, not just informed?

## Target State

A user should be able to:
1. Understand their situation in 30 seconds (acknowledgment + role)
2. Know what to do first in 1 minute (immediate actions)
3. Feel confident about next steps in 3 minutes (full action plan)
4. Generate helpful documents in 5 minutes (templates)
